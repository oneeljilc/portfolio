Hi! My name is Jillian and I am a polymer scientist at Los Alamos National Lab (LANL) in New Mexico. I run a mechanical testing lab where I get paid to crush things and/or pull them apart (Think Hydraulic Press Channel on Youtube). In my lab, we get to study specialty polymer materials for a variety of unique national security applications. It is a fun challenge and every day is different! Approximately 8 years ago, I became interested in data management and got involved in database-building efforts at LANL. Today, I co-lead a team under a digital transformation initiative that is building "AI-ready" material science datasets for the entire US Nuclear Security Enterprise (NSE). It is this passion for data management that has inspired me to pursue a Master's Degree in Data Science from the University of California - San Diego with an expected graduation date of March 2026.
